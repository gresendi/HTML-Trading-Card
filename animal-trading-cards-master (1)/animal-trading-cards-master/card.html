African Savannah Elephant

Amazingly, many elephant calls are too low (15 hertz) for human hearing ability (20 hertz). These infrasonic sounds are capable of traveling long distances.

  • Habitat: Savanna Zones, south of the Sahara Desert
  • Diet: Roots, Grasses, Fruits, and Bark
  • Lifespan: 60-70 years
  • Conservation Status: Vulnerable

The African Savanna elephant is also referred to as the Bush elephant. It can reach a height of up to 7ft, and are very social animals, often traveling in herd of females and adolescents. These elephants are often poached for their Ivory tusks, and are currently in vulnerable status because of it.
